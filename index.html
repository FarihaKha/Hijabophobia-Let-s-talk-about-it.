There are so many topics in the world that we are too afraid to talk about and hijabophobia is one of them, so let’s get comfortable with talking about hijabophobia. My mission is to inform the public about hijabophobia and why you should care about it. But first...


What is Hijabophobia?

The term “hijabophobia” refers to the religious discrimination that Muslims face when wearing an Islamic veil, such as a hijab, chador, niqab, or burqa.

Stereotypes

There are many stereotypes surrounding Muslim women, especially in western societies. These stereotypes cast a negative image on Muslim women. For instance, in many movies and TV shows, Muslim women are typically viewed as "oppressed" by their religion. Having this coverage of Muslims in the media is dangerous because people start to pick up these mindsets and believe that it is true. You have probably even heard some assumptions, such as the fact that all Muslim women are not allowed to drive, forced into marriages, and so much more, even though none of these are true. Muslim women who wear headscarves are targeted the most for incedents of discrimination, even though not all Muslim women wear headscarves. This shows that, as a society, we need to unlearn the stigma and stereotypes that Muslim women have to face everyday.

Bans

If you have been watching the news closely, you might have seen how more and more places are banning the hijab. For example, many workplaces are banning the hijab to achieve "neutrality." How is this so-called act of "neutrality" supposed to justify banning a piece of clothing? This "neutrality" is what causes people to normalize hijabophobia. Workplaces are not the only ones doing this. There are already numerous nations that have banned the burqa. Hijabophobia can even be seen in schools and sports. This is completely unfair because this shows that the world wants people to choose between the religious beliefs and their daily lives.
